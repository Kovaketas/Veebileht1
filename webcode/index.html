Who I am

My name is Berit Aun, and I’m an aspiring artist from the beautiful country of Estonia. Ever since I can remember, I’ve been drawn to creativity and self-expression, and over time, art has become a powerful way for me to explore and communicate my thoughts, feelings, and ideas. Growing up surrounded by Estonia’s rich culture, natural landscapes, and quiet charm has deeply influenced the way I see the world and how I choose to express that vision through my work. I'm still learning and evolving every day, but I'm passionate about developing my skills and finding my unique voice as an artist.


Why I do art

Honestly, the reason I started doing art was because of a competition. I've always had this drive to be the best at everything I try, and when I saw one of my friends drawing, something inside me clicked. I felt this sudden urge to prove that I could do it too. Maybe even better. What started as a challenge quickly became something much more meaningful. As I spent more time with a pencil or brush in hand, I began to truly enjoy the process. There was something calming and rewarding about creating something from nothing, turning a blank page into a reflection of my thoughts and emotions. Over time, that competitive spark turned into a genuine passion, and art became a constant in my life. Something that continues to inspire and motivate me every day.


My art journey

I started my art journey when I was just 10 years old, and ever since then, drawing has been a constant part of my life. It began with simple sketches and doodles, but quickly turned into something much more meaningful. No matter where I was, whether sitting in class, daydreaming with a pencil in hand, or relaxing at home during my free time, I always found myself drawn to art. It became a way for me to express myself, to process the world around me, and to explore my imagination. Over the years, I've continued to practice whenever I get the chance, using any spare moment to improve my skills and challenge myself creatively. What started as a hobby has grown into a true passion, and I can't imagine my life without it.


My art process

I don't follow the traditional route of digital art. Rather I do digital painting which is more akind to traditional paintings. The main benefit of using digital software for drawing is the layer function, but I keep it to the minimum for my art process. It's not that I don't use it, I just don't see the need of using it that much. Naturally with everything on the same layer the process becomes a bit messy, that's the kind of look I am trying to achieve.
